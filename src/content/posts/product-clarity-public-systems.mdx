Public-facing systems carry a different burden. They must work for people who did not choose the system and may not trust it yet.

That changes the product brief:

1. The interface must explain the system's intent.
2. The default path must be resilient to stress.
3. The language must reduce fear without minimizing complexity.

This is where institutional design and product thinking meet. The goal is not frictionless flow at all costs. The goal is reliable progress under real-world constraints.
