Writing is not a side activity to strategy. It is the mechanism that makes strategy auditable.

In fast-moving environments, spoken decisions decay quickly. Written decisions travel further, survive turnover, and reveal assumptions that would otherwise remain hidden.

Three habits have proven consistently useful:

- Write the decision, not just the discussion.
- Name the trade-offs explicitly.
- Capture what would change your mind.

This is as true for public-facing systems as it is for early-stage platforms.
